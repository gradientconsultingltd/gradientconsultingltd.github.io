Privacy Statement - GradientXchange

Privacy Statement for Users

Quick Overview

Why we collect your information

Our main purpose in requesting your personal details is to provide you with a smooth and consistent experience when using our platform and when accessing the fiat-to-crypto conversion services ("Fiat Gateways") of your choice. By sharing your details with our trusted Fiat Gateway partners, you avoid the need to re-enter the same information multiple times.

Please note: Fiat Gateways are independently responsible for carrying out Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) checks in line with applicable laws. These processes are managed directly by the Fiat Gateways, not by GradientXchange, and we do not store or process KYC/AML data.

What information we collect

We gather both personal and non-personal information. Non-personal data may include transaction activity and how you use our services. Personal information includes your wallet address and email.

You also have rights granted under applicable law, including:

  • Right to know what information we hold
  • Right to request corrections or deletion of your data
  • Right to object to processing in certain circumstances

We apply strict safeguards to protect all personal data against misuse or unauthorized access.

When we collect data

You may be asked to provide or update your details when registering or using our platform, whether through our website or mobile app.

Who is responsible

GradientXchange (Gradient Consulting Limited) acts as the data controller for personal data we collect in relation to your account and our services. Data handled by Fiat Gateways falls under their own privacy policies.

We may also share limited information with affiliated companies (e.g., for customer support) or carefully chosen subcontractors under strict contractual agreements.

How long we keep data

We retain information only as long as required to maintain your account, fulfill our services, comply with legal obligations, and resolve disputes.

Glossary of Terms

(Examples)

  • Personal Data – any information that can identify an individual.
  • Data Controller – the entity deciding how and why data is processed.
  • Data Processor – an entity processing data on behalf of a controller.
  • Anonymization – altering data so individuals cannot be identified.
  • Profiling – automated processing to predict or evaluate personal aspects.
  • PEP (Politically Exposed Person) – individuals in prominent public roles subject to enhanced screening.
